About

I earned my B.S. in Environmental Science and a Technical Certificate in Geographic Information Science (GIS) at the University of Massachusetts Amherst from 2016-2019. During my time as an undergrad, I worked as a research assistant both in a biogeochemistry lab and a viticulture lab, while also working as a teaching assistant for a GIS course.

After graduation, I was a participant at the NASA DEVELOP Program's Boston node. During this time, my team and I examined tick-borne illness risk and emergence by evaluating land cover change and tick habitat suitability in southern Maine using NASA Earth data. Hosted on Google Earth Engine, our web application uses a machine learning algorithm that creates a supervised land cover classification to assist users with identifying areas prone to tick exposure.
