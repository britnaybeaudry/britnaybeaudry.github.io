About

I earned my B.S. in Environmental Science and a Technical Certificate in Geographic Information Science (GIS) at the University of Massachusetts Amherst from 2016-2019. During my time as an undergrad, I worked as a research assistant both in a biogeochemistry lab and a viticulture lab, while also working as a teaching assistant for a GIS course.

After graduation, I was a participant at the NASA DEVELOP Program's Boston node. During this time, my team and I examined tick-borne illness risk and emergence by evaluating land cover change and tick habitat suitability in southern Maine using NASA Earth data. Hosted on Google Earth Engine, our web application uses a machine learning algorithm that creates a supervised land cover classification to assist users with identifying areas prone to tick exposure. Feel free to check out the webpage about this project here.

The following summer, I returned to DEVELOP as an Assistant Geoinformatics Fellow out of Ames Research Center. In this role, I worked program-wide to assist with code development and troubleshooting issues relating to GIS, virtual machines, and online tools.

From here I was hired as the Geoinformatics Fellow for DEVELOP at Ames. In this position, I work with teams on 10-week feasibility studies that use NASA Earth Observations to address environmental issues to community concerns around the globe. Some examples of the projects we have completed can be found here. My personal research interests surround aquatic remote sensing, environmental data science, and machine learning.
